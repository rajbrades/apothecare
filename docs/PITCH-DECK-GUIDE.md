## Slide 13: Regulatory & Risk Awareness

**Headline:** We've Thought About This

**Content:**

### FDA Classification
Apothecare operates as a **Clinical Decision Support (CDS)** tool under the 21st Century Cures Act exemption criteria (Section 3060). The platform:
1. Is not intended to replace clinical judgment — it supports and augments practitioner decision-making
2. Displays underlying evidence and citations so practitioners can independently review the basis for any recommendation
3. Does not acquire, process, or analyze medical images or signals from a medical device
4. Is designed for healthcare professionals with the training to independently evaluate the output

We are monitoring FDA's evolving guidance on AI/ML-based CDS and will engage regulatory counsel as the product scales.

### Liability Position
- All AI recommendations are presented as clinical decision *support*, not clinical decisions
- Practitioners retain full clinical authority — every recommendation can be overridden, modified, or rejected
- Clinician action overrides create an audit trail of practitioner judgment
- Terms of service clearly delineate AI as an assistive tool

### HIPAA Compliance
- Row-Level Security on all 25+ tables (database-enforced, not application-layer)
- HIPAA audit logging on every PHI access
- BAA-ready (Supabase Pro includes HIPAA BAA)
- Pursuing BAAs with AI providers (OpenAI, Anthropic) — both offer HIPAA-eligible tiers
- SOC 2 Type I certification planned for Month 6

### AI Provider Risk
- Multi-LLM architecture eliminates single-provider dependency
- Automatic failover: OpenAI → Anthropic → MiniMax
- No patient PHI sent to AI providers in identifiable form (de-identification layer planned for enterprise tier)

# Critical Pitching Tips

### 1. Lead with "This Is Not a Mockup"
Your single biggest advantage over every other seed-stage deck: **the product works.** Say it in the first 30 seconds. Most seed decks show Figma. You show production software.

### 2. Nail the openEvidence Comp
"We are openEvidence but for the $160B supplement and functional diagnostic market" is your most powerful hook. Lead with it in conversations. It immediately communicates the business model and scale.

### 3. Get 10 Practitioners Using It Before You Pitch
Nothing replaces real user quotes. Even 2 weeks of practitioner usage gives you testimonials, usage data, and feedback. This is the single most impactful thing you can do.

### 4. Practice the Demo Transition
Practice switching from deck to app 100 times. Have a backup video. Best flows:
- **60-sec lab flow:** PDF drag → extraction → dual ranges → trends → push to timeline

### 5. The IFM Matrix Is Your Secret Weapon
When a functional medicine KOL sees you've operationalized the IFM Matrix in software, they immediately understand the depth. No one else has done this.

### 6. Address Solo Founder Risk Head-On
"I built this entire platform alone in [X] weeks. That's the execution speed you're investing in. The $1M hires 2 engineers and a growth lead — I've already proven I can ship."

### 7. Have Unit Economics Ready
- **CAC:** Free tier eliminates traditional CAC. Estimated <$50 for paid conversion via organic word-of-mouth.

### 8. Know Your Lab Vendor Coverage
When asked: Quest, LabCorp, Diagnostic Solutions, Genova, Precision Analytical, Mosaic Diagnostics, Vibrant, SpectraCell, RealTime Labs, ZRT Laboratory.

### 9. Anticipate Regulatory Questions

### 10. Frame Defensibility as Compounding
Don't say "we built more features." Say "every practitioner who uses Apothecare makes the platform smarter. After 10,000 protocols, we'll have the largest dataset of functional medicine clinical decision patterns in existence. That's the moat."
