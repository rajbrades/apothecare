Critical Pitching Tips

Preparation notes — do not distribute to investors

1. Lead with "This Is Not a Mockup"

Say it in the first 30 seconds. Most seed decks show Figma. You show production software. 300+ files, 60+ endpoints, live AI.

2. Nail the openEvidence Comp

"We are openEvidence but for the $160B supplement and functional diagnostic market." This immediately communicates business model and scale.

3. Get 10 Practitioners Using It Before You Pitch

Nothing replaces real user quotes. Even 2 weeks of practitioner usage gives you testimonials, usage data, and feedback. This is the single most impactful thing you can do.

4. Practice the Demo Transition

  • 60-sec lab: PDF drag → extraction → dual ranges → trends → push to timeline
  • 90-sec visit: Record → transcribe → generate → Visit AI Assistant → push protocol
  • 30-sec chat: Clinical Lens "Both" → complex question → comparison card + evidence badges

5. IFM Matrix Is Your Secret Weapon

When a FM KOL sees you've operationalized the IFM Matrix in software, they immediately understand the depth. No one else has done this.

6. Address Solo Founder Risk

"I built this alone in [X] weeks. That's the execution speed you're investing in. The $1M hires 2 engineers and a growth lead."

7. Unit Economics

  • CAC: Free tier → organic WoM. <$50 for paid conversion.
  • LTV: $89/mo × 24 months = $2,136 + dispensary affiliate.
  • Margin: SaaS 70-80%. Sponsored pathways 90%+.
  • Comp: Cerbo charges $150-400/mo for basic EHR. $89/mo is under-priced.

8. Anticipate Regulatory Questions

Have your CDS exemption framing ready (Cures Act, Section 3060, 4 criteria). Mention evolving FDA guidance and regulatory counsel at scale.

9. Frame Defensibility as Compounding

Don't say "we built more features." Say "every practitioner who uses Apothecare makes the platform smarter. After 10K protocols, we'll have the largest FM clinical decision dataset. That's the moat."
