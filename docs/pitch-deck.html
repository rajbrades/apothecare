The Problem

Integrative Practitioners Are Drowning in Complexity
  1. Documentation Overload: Clinicians spend too much time charting and too little time practicing medicine. Today's workflows are built around billing requirements, not clinical reasoning.
  2. Fragmented Lab Intelligence: Clinicians must synthesize biomarker data across multiple vendors, formats, and reference ranges, making interpretation slow, inconsistent, and vulnerable to missed insights.
  3. Supplement and Interaction Complexity: Building effective protocols requires checking drug-supplement, supplement-supplement, and condition-based interactions across fragmented sources. The process is manual, time-consuming, and carries real safety risk.
  4. The Education-to-Execution Gap: Clinicians are flooded with new frameworks, therapies, and protocols, but lack the infrastructure to turn what they learn into structured, repeatable patient care.
  5. A Missing Clinical Workflow Layer: Integrative medicine lacks a dedicated workflow layer to connect insight, decision-making, safety, and execution at the point of care.
  6. The Monday Morning Gap: Clinicians leave seminars energized, but by Monday the realities of time pressure, charting, and fragmented workflows push new learning back into old habits.

Regulatory & Risk

We've Thought About This

FDA Classification

Apothecare qualifies for the Clinical Decision Support exemption under the 21st Century Cures Act (Section 3060). We meet all 4 required criteria:

  • Does not acquire, process, or analyze medical images or signals
  • Displays and analyzes clinical information for healthcare professionals
  • Supports — does not replace — clinical judgment
  • Every recommendation is transparent and independently reviewable (citations, evidence badges, source attribution)

This means no 510(k) clearance required — we can move fast while remaining fully compliant. Regulatory counsel will be retained as we scale to stay ahead of evolving FDA AI/ML guidance.

Liability Position

  • AI is decision support, not decision making
  • Practitioners retain full clinical authority
  • Clinician override audit trail
  • ToS clearly delineates AI as assistive

2. Nail the OpenEvidence Comp

"We are OpenEvidence but for the $160B supplement and functional diagnostic market." This immediately communicates business model and scale.

3. Get 10 Practitioners Using It Before You Pitch

Nothing replaces real user quotes. Even 2 weeks of practitioner usage gives you testimonials, usage data, and feedback. This is the single most impactful thing you can do.

4. Practice the Demo Transition

  • 60-sec lab: PDF drag → extraction → dual ranges → trends → push to timeline
  • 90-sec visit: Record → transcribe → generate → Visit AI Assistant → push protocol
  • 30-sec chat: Clinical Lens "Both" → complex question → comparison card + evidence badges

8. Anticipate Regulatory Questions
