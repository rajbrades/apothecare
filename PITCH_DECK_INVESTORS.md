## Slide 5: The Three Compounding Moats

**Headline:** Why This Cannot Be Copied in 18 Months

**Moat 1 — The Clinical Intelligence Graph**
Every protocol decision, pattern match, and clinical outcome logged in Apothecare aggregates into a proprietary outcomes graph. By year 3, Apothecare knows which protocols actually work for which biomarker patterns in which patient phenotypes — at a population level. No individual practitioner knows this. No textbook knows this. Only Apothecare. This graph becomes a licensing asset to pharma and supplement brands, the foundation for clinical trial recruitment, and eventually the evidence base for insurance reimbursement.

**Moat 2 — Protocol Network Effects**
When practitioners publish, share, and refine protocols inside Apothecare, their intellectual work and practice history live here. Leaving means leaving their own outcomes data behind. This is data gravity — the same lock-in that makes GitHub irreplaceable for developers.

**Moat 3 — Regulatory Positioning**
We will pursue FDA 510(k) clearance on specific safety-critical tools: the interaction checker, contraindication screening, and red flag detection. FDA clearance takes 3–5 years and significant capital. Once cleared, competitors face a multi-year, multi-million dollar barrier. Health systems and payors require cleared tools. We get there first.

**Speaker Notes:** "The SaaS subscription is our wedge, not our destination. Every practitioner who subscribes feeds the Clinical Intelligence Graph. The graph is the business."

## Appendix A: The MCP Architecture

Apothecare is built on a five-server Model Context Protocol architecture that acts as the intelligence orchestration layer between the provider interface and all clinical data and logic.

**Five MCP Servers:**
1. **Patient Context Server** — demographics, labs, meds, timelines, symptom scores
2. **Clinical Logic Server** — pattern matching, protocol libraries, retesting logic, IFM Matrix
3. **Safety Engine Server** — interaction checks, contraindications, red flags, scope guardrails
4. **Documentation & Workflow Server** — SOAP drafts, AVS generation, follow-up tasks
5. **Evidence & Research Server** — PubMed retrieval, internal monographs, citation grounding

**Provider request flow:**
1. Retrieve patient summary, labs, meds, and timeline
2. Match dominant clinical patterns
3. Generate candidate next-step options
4. Run safety and contraindication screening
5. Pull supporting evidence where needed
6. Draft provider note, plan summary, and AVS
7. Require human review before any irreversible action

## Appendix B: Why Not Start From Scratch?

The existing Apothecare codebase represents approximately 18 months of equivalent engineering work:
- Visit workspace, IFM Matrix mapping, AI Scribe, SOAP generation
- Multi-vendor lab parsing with functional range intelligence
- Supplement intelligence with interaction checking and brand formulary
- RAG pipeline with PubMed ingestion and vector search
- Full patient chart management with 8-tab view and FM Timeline
- Supabase schema with RLS, HIPAA-compliant data handling

The tech stack (Next.js 15, Supabase, Claude, Whisper) is correct for the platform vision.
What is being added is the infrastructure underneath (audit logging, outcomes tracking, protocol versioning) and the platform layer on top (marketplace, API economy, patient network).

This is additive architecture, not a rewrite.
